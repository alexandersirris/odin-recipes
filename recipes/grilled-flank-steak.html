Grilled Flank Steak

Flank steak is the cow's lower chest or abdominal muscle. Since it's muscle that's used a lot, the flank is a thick-grained, low-fat cut. The beauty of it is that it's inexpensive, but flavorful. It's a versatile cut of beef, especially when prepared well. It's best marinated and then seared whole over high heat. Cutting the cooked flank steak against the grain at a 45 degree angle ensures you're cutting through the tough parts, making it easier to eat. Skirt steak is a similar cut, but has a different shape, a bit more fat, and the grain runs in a different direction. No matter the occassion, when you're thinking of the amount of flank steak to cook don't forget all the left over dishes you can create.

  1. Use them as filling for weeknight tacos.
  2. Add them to stir-fried vegetables.
  3. Toss them with ramen and broccoli, like this recipe.
  4. Add them to a bowl of ramen or pho.
  5. Make a steak salad with the slices.
  6. Toss them in a grain bowl.
  7. Use them as a burrito filling.
  8. Make nachos.
  9. Serve them on the side for DIY lettuce wraps.
  10. Make homemade cup noodles.

Ingrediants

For the marinade
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

For the steak
  • 1 (2-pound) flank steak
  • Coarse salt, to taste (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste (optional)
